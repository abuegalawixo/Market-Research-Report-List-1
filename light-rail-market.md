<p><ul><li>Low Capacity Light Rail</li><li>High Capacity Light Rail</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Light rail markets can be categorized into low capacity and high capacity types. Low capacity light rail systems are designed for shorter routes and lower passenger volumes, typically serving urban areas with less demand. They often feature smaller vehicles and shorter headways. In contrast, high capacity light rail systems accommodate larger passenger volumes, often operating on longer routes with more frequent service. These systems utilize larger vehicles and can integrate elements of heavy rail to manage increased demand effectively.</p></p>
