<p><p>The venous blood collection tube market is primarily segmented into glass and plastic types. In contrast, plastic tubes, often made from materials like polyethylene or polypropylene, are lighter, shatterproof, and generally more cost-effective, offering greater convenience for high-volume collections. Each type serves specific needs in clinical and laboratory settings, influencing choice based on safety, cost, and application.</p></p>

<p><p>The venous blood collection tube market includes various applications based on additives like anticoagulants, coagulants, and no additives. Anticoagulant tubes are crucial for tests requiring blood to remain fluid, preventing clotting during analysis. Coagulant tubes, on the other hand, facilitate clot formation, ideal for serum tests. Tubes with no additives are used for straightforward sample collection, where test results are unaffected by any chemical agents. Together, these categories cater to diverse laboratory needs in diagnostics and research.</p></p>
